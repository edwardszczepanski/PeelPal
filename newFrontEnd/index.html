Enli Health App

Healthcare app in C# and XAML

Previous Next


About the Enli Heatlh Intelligence Internship
Formerly the Kryptiq Corporation, Enli is a healthcare software company that specializes in health management systems. Much of their products revolve around Electronic Medical Records and data integration.
I worked 300 hours in total for the firm as a full-time intern during the Summer of 2013. My role as a software engineer was to create a new Windows mobile application with the mentorship of a Senior Software developer.
In a short amount of time I created a Windows mobile application that was intended for healthcare patients. It would allow people to better interact with their care professionals and help expand Kryptiq's expansion onto mobile platforms.

Learning Curve
When I applied to this internship I was told I was going to create a web application using JavaScript and HTML. For weeks leading up to the first I day I practiced these languages by developing small web apps. On my first day on the job my mentor informed me that plans had changed and I would be assigned to a new project. My task for the Summer would be to build a fully-fledged Windows Application using C# and XAML using the .NET framework. At that point I had no prior experiences in any of those technologies. In the following weeks I spent countless hours, on the job and off, learning the new programming environment and made real progress on my application. I would have meetings with my mentor and with the general company where I would take notes making sure to absorb as much information as possible.

Development
Most of my work day involved programming and implementing new features into the application. Through this internship I learned about the standards that industry has for software development. The first invaluable skill was the ability to work with version control systems. This picture shows a snapshot of the commits for this project with a diagram for all the branching. I used Mercurial and TortoiseHG to accomplish the version control for this application. Another great thing that I learned was the use of debugging mode to help find the issues in code. In addition, I implemented unit testing to ensure that my application was manipulating data correctly. I had code review sessions with my mentor to ensure that my code was implementing best practices such as proper naming conventions, modularity and readability.

The App
After many revisions and a lot of hard work I was finally able to deploy my mobile application in a closed environment. Being able to work with my functional application on a separate platform was an extremely gratifying experience. The embedded video embedded shows the app function.
Features:
• Review and Send Messages from Care Professionals
• Receive attachments such as Medical Charts
• Review and Cancel Appointments
• Review medications
• Bing Maps API Integration

Presentation
After I had completed my internship I created a presentation of my experience working at the Kryptiq Corporation. Much of my material can be found on this page except I want further in depth into the earlier versions of my application. This speech was given to the entire Kryptiq Corporation which was fantastic as I was able to communicate my work to others and practice my presentation skills. I gave a very similar presentation at the Apprenticeships for Science and Engineering Symposium where I created a trifold board that highlighted my work. This experience solidified my interest in software and has directed my career to where it is today.

  • Date: Summer 2013

Team Henry's Full Circle

Full Circle
Full Circle is a circle based rhythm game that uses a dance pad to interact with the player. Consider it a hybrid of Guitar Hero, Dance Dance Revolution, and something completely new. Complete with an arcade and story mode. Full Circle was built from the ground up using Java, Slick2D graphics library, and the Eclipse IDE. The dance pad was built from various woods and acrylic materials with an Arduino used to interface with the PC. The game was a great success, winning the "Best in Show" award at the Oregon Game Project Challenge. There was a lot of interest in this game, which led it to be showcased at the Pensole Design Academy and eventually the Portland Art Museum.

Team
The Full Circle team emerged from the Computer Science Department of Wilson High School in Portland, Oregon. The three members of the team included myself, Kyle Sautter, and David Anuta. We each brought different skills to the group. My role was to work on software, the design and building of the dance pad, and all public relations. Kyle's main role included art and assets, creating much of the sprites and music. David contributed as software developer, with his work including the Arduino integration. Our game is RGB (Red Green Blue) themed, and the accompanying photo shows each of us with our corresponding shirts. I am in the center with David on the left and Kyle on the right.

Game Concept
When we sought out to create a rhythm game we had many ideas. We knew we wanted to create something unique that had never been done before. After a few revisions we worked out a system where circles would appear and move clockwise on different radial circles. When the circles arrived at the 3 o'clock position a user was supposed to hit the corresponding color on the dance pad. What makes this special is that a player's perception of the timing is thrown off by the configuration of the circles. The outer circles appear to move faster than the inner ones although they are all radially travelling at the same speed. These mechanics create a very fun and challenging dance game.

Dance Pad
Not only was Full Circle impressive from a software point of view, the integration of the dance pad hardware is what made the game really stand out. The dance pad was made of a wooden frame with three openings for each individual button. Underneath each pad was velostat, a pressure-sensitive conductive sheet that, when pressed, sent signals to an Arduino. The software was programmed to detect a certain amount of pressure. The photo here shows the pad in the middle of the painting and building process.

Execution
The development of Full Circle took place over the span of two years. The coordination was made possible through the Honors Computer Science courses that the team took together. Simple version control methods were utilized where team members would work on individual pieces of the program. An example of this is when I programmed a separate menu system that was later integrated into the main program. We have since implemented better practices, utilizing GitHub properly, for our version control. One challenge was the use of the Slick2D library that is no longer supported. This made even finding documentation a challenge, which meant that many issues had to be worked out independently.

Finished Product
Though this describes the background of Full Circle, the video embedded in this slider provides a clear explanation of what was achieved. The trailer demonstrates parts of the gameplay and showcases the dance pad. Full Circle was further improved for the Mythos Challenge, held at the Portland Art Museum. The software was updated to be more stable and a completely new dance pad was completed with a stunningly thin design and more sensitive detection.

JT Johnson & Sons Internship

A Summer in Australia

Previous Next


Kapunda
About an hour NE from Adelaide, Kapunda is situated in an area of significant agricultural resources, featuring wheat, oats and wine production. With a population now of just over 3,000, Kapunda started as a mining town in mid-1800s. Aussie rules football and cricket make up much of the entertainment for the town.

JT Johnson & Sons
Founded in 1923, JT Johnson’s & Sons is one of Australia’s premier agricultural exporters, producing animal feed in the form of pellets, hay and straw. JTJ’s innovative approach and commitment to quality has distinguished it as a global supplier to markets in the Middle East and throughout Asia.

My Role
I interned during the Australian “winter” of 2014, developing a firsthand understanding of international trade by generating an internal allocation system, creating product guides and conducting market research.

Market Research
Doing research on the Japanese market gave me a chance to learn new perspectives of a country I’ve lived in and visited several times. Agriculture is an intensely competitive, low-margin business, and is very sensitive to small price differences. Research that leads to new insights can mean the difference between profit and loss. Analyzing customs data I generated tables breaking down imports by product and port for the entire country, identifying sales trends and highlighting new opportunities for growth.

Product Guide
JT Johnson’s sells a number of products, with each having different grades. This variety provides the customer the chance to match the exact product to the particular situation, but it can be difficult to understand all the different choices. I was tasked with developing JTJ’s first product guide, which required me to confer with a number of staff to understand each product, its key sales point, and its different grades. I made sure the guide was not just factually correct, but designed in a way that made it easy to read and to reference. The finished guide has helped customers better understand their options, and has even been used to train new employees.

Community
My experience in Australia was extremely memorable because of how welcoming the community was. During my time there I stayed with JTJ employees, allowing me to fully soak up the culture. In Kapunda I played Australian Rules Football with the local team, the Bombers. Going to practice and competing with locals who were around my age made me feel like I was a part of the community. Kapunda is my “Australian hometown” and when I return I look forward to playing another game of footy.

  • Date: Summer 2014
